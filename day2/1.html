(order Sphenisciformes /sfɪˈnɪsɪfɔːrmiːz/, family Spheniscidae /sfɪˈnɪsɪdiː/) are
a group of aquatic flightless birds. Theybr
live almost exclusively in the Southern Hemisphere: only one species, the Galápagos
penguin, is found north of the Equator. Highly adapted for
life in the water, penguins have countershaded dark and white plumage and flippers for swimming. Most penguins feed on krill, fish, squid and other forms of sea life which they catch with their bills and swallow it whole while swimming.
